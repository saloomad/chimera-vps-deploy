# Macro Analyst

Use this skill when the question is really about the environment around crypto, not just the chart in front of us.

## Purpose

- turn macro signals into a simple market backdrop
- explain whether the environment is helping, hurting, or neutral for crypto risk
- translate cross-asset moves into direct implications for BTC, majors, and alt risk
- answer what Deezoh should do with that macro read right now

## Section Boundary

- `Part 6: Macro And Cross-Asset`
  - economic calendar
  - rates and yields
  - SPX / DXY / gold / oil context
  - BTC.D / USDT.D / TOTAL3 / OTHERS context
  - recommendation weight and plain-English context for Deezoh
- `Part 7: News And Catalysts`
  - live headlines
  - symbol-specific catalysts
  - company earnings
  - macro events only when they are acting like a live headline catalyst

## Core Workflow

1. Identify the macro window.
   - note the next important event or the most recent high-impact print
   - call out whether the market is waiting for data, reacting to data, or in a quiet gap
2. Read the major macro drivers.
   - rates and central-bank tone
   - inflation and growth data
   - dollar strength
   - equities and broad risk appetite
   - global liquidity or tightening signals when relevant
3. Convert macro into a trading backdrop.
   - do not stop at labels like `mixed` or `caution`
   - explain what the backdrop means for BTC versus broad alts
   - say whether macro should be used as context, caution, or conviction support
   - whether the setup favors BTC quality, majors, or broad alt participation
4. State what would change the read.
   - next event, invalidation, or missing confirmation
5. Use the preferred visual board when available.
   - `https://www.tradingview.com/chart/81pv7c9g/`
   - read `1W`, `1D`, `4H`, `1H`
   - answer what SPX, DXY, gold, oil, USDT.D, and OTHERS add that the file-backed lanes do not

## Output Contract

- lead with the macro verdict in plain English
- compare bullish, bearish, and no-trade interpretations when the read is mixed
- keep macro separate from technicals, derivatives, or liquidation unless you are explicitly cross-checking them
- say when the macro signal is weak, stale, or overridden by event risk
- explicitly answer:
  - what this means for BTC
  - what this means for alts
  - what Deezoh should watch next
  - what is still missing or only partially covered

## Do Not

- do not present macro as a precise timing signal by itself
- do not collapse all assets into one blanket crypto conclusion
- do not skip the no-trade path when the macro evidence is conflicted
- do not put earnings into Section 6 unless they matter only as broad equity-week context
