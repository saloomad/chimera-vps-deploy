# Bitcoin Swarm Case Study

Updated: 2026-04-30

## Why This Example Matters

The Bitcoin example is the clearest concrete proof of how the Kimi-style swarm was actually turned from a user prompt into a full research and writing pipeline.

## Original User Ask

In plain English, the user wanted:

- Bitcoin analyst ratings
- technical analysis across timeframes
- best Bitcoin entries
- best altcoins to trade and why

That is the kind of request that really does justify a research swarm.

## The Plan Structure Used

The saved `plan.md` used a three-stage layout:

### Objective

Deliver a comprehensive report covering:

1. Bitcoin analyst ratings
2. multi-timeframe technical analysis
3. best BTC entry levels
4. best altcoins with rationale

### Stage 1 - Deep Research

The example loaded `deep-research-swarm` and split the job into parallel research slices:

- Agent A: analyst ratings and sentiment
- Agent B: BTC multi-timeframe technical analysis
- Agent C: best altcoins and narratives
- Agent D: macro and crypto market context

### Stage 2 - Synthesis And Report Writing

The example then loaded a writing skill and consolidated the research into a structured markdown report.

### Stage 3 - DOCX Conversion

The example then loaded a docx skill and exported the final report.

## What The More Detailed Notion Trace Added

The Notion trace showed that the real execution expanded the research into a much richer decomposition:

- 11 to 12 research dimensions
- one sub-agent per dimension
- separate chapter writers later
- final docx conversion as a separate step

## Dimension Set Used In Practice

The detailed example broke the work into dimensions such as:

1. BTC short-term technical analysis
2. BTC medium-term technical analysis
3. BTC long-term technical analysis
4. BTC entry strategies and risk management
5. institutional analyst ratings
6. on-chain and derivatives data
7. macro and Fed impact
8. Layer 1 and DeFi altcoins
9. meme, AI, and narrative altcoins
10. BTC dominance and altseason timing
11. quantum or black swan risk
12. trading strategies and portfolio construction

## Writing Pipeline Used In Practice

The finished report was then assembled into chapters including:

- market snapshot and analyst consensus
- Bitcoin technical analysis
- best Bitcoin entry strategies
- best altcoins to trade
- risk factors and black swans
- trading strategies and portfolio construction

## Final Report Shape

The generated docx showed a final structure with:

- executive summary
- multi-timeframe BTC analysis
- entry zones
- altcoin selection
- risk matrix
- trading strategies and allocation frameworks

## Transferable Lessons

### What To Keep

- write the plan first
- separate research from writing
- keep the report chapters separate from the research dimensions
- preserve the export step as its own final phase when needed

### What To Generalize

The exact BTC dimensions are not universal.
The real reusable pattern is:

- timeframes
- stakeholder views
- analytical methods
- scenario risks
- ecosystem rotation

### What To Avoid

- forcing every deep research job into the exact same BTC dimensions
- using the full swarm when a normal loop or lean live pipeline is enough
